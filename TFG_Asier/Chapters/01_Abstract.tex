\chapter{Abstract} \label{ch:chapter_01}
	
This Bachelor’s Thesis focuses on implementing post-processing tools for the thermal analysis 
of space systems, leveraging results obtained from the \acrshort{esatan-tms} software. 

Building upon a previous study limited to a steady-state radiative case, the main objective is to evaluate the dynamic 
thermal behavior of the system by applying a fully transient model. 

Since \acrshort{esatan-tms} 
presents limitations regarding integrated data visualization, this project develops custom post-processing 
routines in Python, leveraging libraries such as NumPy, Pandas, and Matplotlib. These tools automate 
the extraction of nodal data to generate advanced graphical representations, including temperature evolution 
curves and transient heat fluxes over time. Ultimately, this methodology optimizes the interpretation of 
complex thermal results, facilitating the comparison of different scenarios and ensuring the system's 
performance under realistic orbital thermal cycles.
